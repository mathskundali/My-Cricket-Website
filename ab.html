AB de Villiers

"Mr. 360° – Revolutionizing Cricket Forever"

Biography

Abraham Benjamin de Villiers, popularly known as AB de Villiers, is one of the greatest cricketers of all time. Born on 17 February 1984 in Pretoria, South Africa, he displayed extraordinary talent from a young age, quickly rising through the ranks of domestic cricket to make a lasting impact on the international stage.

Known for his versatility and unmatched innovation, AB de Villiers earned the nickname "Mr. 360" for his ability to play shots all around the wicket. His aggressive yet calculated batting style made him a formidable opponent in both Tests and limited-overs formats.

Career Achievements

Throughout his illustrious career, AB de Villiers has achieved numerous milestones, including:

  • Over 8,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs) with an astonishing average of 53.5.
  • Fastest 50, 100, and 150 in ODI history.
  • Capable of changing the course of a match in just a few overs.
  • Highly successful IPL career with Royal Challengers Bangalore, becoming a fan favorite for his explosive batting.
  • A true ambassador of sportsmanship and innovation in cricket.

Memorable Moments

AB de Villiers has been part of countless iconic moments in cricket history:

  • Scoring the fastest 50 in just 16 balls against West Indies in 2015.
  • Historic 149-ball 278 against Pakistan in 2010.
  • Match-winning partnerships that have turned impossible games into victories.
  • His leadership as captain and vice-captain of the South African national team.
