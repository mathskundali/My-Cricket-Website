Early Life & Background

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India. From a very young age, he displayed extraordinary passion for cricket. His father, Prem Kohli, supported his dream and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy. Domestic & U-19 Career

Virat Kohli first gained national attention after captaining India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 World Cup. His leadership qualities, consistency, and fearless batting style made him stand out among his peers.

International Debut

Kohli made his ODI debut in 2008 against Sri Lanka. Though his early career had ups and downs, his dedication, fitness, and mental strength helped him become one of the most reliable batsmen in world cricket.

Batting Style & Technique

Virat Kohli is known for his technically sound batting, aggressive mindset, and ability to chase targets under pressure. His cover drives, wrist work, and running between wickets are considered among the best in cricket history.

Records & Achievements

Virat Kohli holds numerous records across formats including fastest centuries, most runs in chases, and consistent performances in ICC tournaments. He is often compared with legends like Sachin Tendulkar.

Captaincy Era

As captain, Kohli transformed the Indian team into a dominant force, especially in Test cricket. His emphasis on fitness, aggression, and fast bowling revolutionized Indian cricket.

IPL Career

Virat Kohli has been the face of Royal Challengers Bangalore since the beginning of IPL. He holds the record for most runs in a single IPL season (973 runs in 2016).

Fitness & Lifestyle

Kohli is known for his strict fitness regime, clean diet, and dedication to maintaining peak physical condition. He inspired a fitness culture among Indian cricketers.

Legacy & Impact

Virat Kohli is not just a cricketer; he is an icon. His influence goes beyond cricket, inspiring millions with his work ethic, passion, and never-give-up attitude.
